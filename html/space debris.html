Space Debris

Introduction

One of the major issues we face regarding space exploration and space travel in general is that our aircrafts and or vehicles are polluting space throught space debris, or more commonly known space junk.Any human made object that is in orbir and has no serving purpose is considered as space debris. Space debris can vary from old satellites to very small particles of paint or aircraft parts. We estimate that we have around 25000 visible space debris objects that can be tracked . Those objects pose a real threat for the working equipments that is currently operating in space and also for future endeavors. To understand more about this occuring past and future issue we have to see the history of space exploration and the reasons that made space pollution a problem.

Space Race Era

Throught the 50s and the 60s space exploratoion became a race between the us and ussr and the first attempts to reach space had taken place and the first satelite was sent into space , the historic Sputnik 1 that launched in 1957. Sputnik 1 was the first human made space debri that reached space , as Sputnik was made of 2 parts one being the artificial satelite throught the rocket stage and the main part the satellite itself. This made scientists sceptical about the potential issues of space debris and the need to keep track of them was more relevant than ever. Use of radars that was used by the military helped with the track of those debris as space exploration was begining to take place and pieces of destroyed or malfunctiong satelites and aircraft started to occupy earths orbit. Certain projects have been created such as such as the North American Air Defense’s (NORAD) known as the North American Aerospace Defense Command— Space Object Catalog in order to improve upon the foundation that Project Space Track set. In 1961 Thor-Ablestar rocket upper stage exploded and created at least 200 cataloged fragments and was the first major event that created manyt scape debris.

70s 80s and 90s

In the 70s and the 80s space exploration scyrocketed and more so it was natural for more programs and initiatives to appear to help with the space debris issues . For example, NASA’s creation of what is now the Orbital Debris Program (OPDO) in 1979, also OPDO began conferring with the European Space Agency about debris in 1987. the Air Force Space Debris Research Program was established in the 1980s. The research program began as multiple fragmentation events of Delta rocket systems occurred and the U.S. forayed into ASAT testing activities. In 1993 the Inter-Agency Space Debris Coordination Committee (IADC) was founded in order to implement some guidelines and talk about these issues.Furthermore In 1997 the the Center for Orbital and Reentry Debris Studies (CORDS) was established.CORDS was able to predict reentries since the late 1990s and was able to document spacecraft and other objects that have reentered the Earth’s atmosphere since 2000.
