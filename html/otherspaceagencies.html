    • ESA is a space exploration company in Europe with its headquartes in Paris, France. It was founded on the 30th of May 1975, at that time only 10 countries where members (Belgium, Spain, United Kingdom, Italy, Denmark, France, Sweden, the Netherlands, Switzerland and Germany.) ESA's first ever mission was the Cos-B that launched on the 9th of August 1975, its mission was to study cosmic gamma-rays. The Cos-B mission was completed on April 25th 1982 ten years after its launch.

    Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA):

    The Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA) is a space agency that was founded on October 1st 2003, its located Chofu, Tokyo. Its job is to launch satellites into orbit and develop new techonologies for more up-to-date missions. In 2012 JAXA started to help the military with techologies that would alert them if a missile would approach (missile early warnings).

    China National Space Administration (CNSA):

    The China National Space Administration (CNSA) is a space agency that was created in 1993. It is the first agency that managed to the far side of the moon with its robotic spaceship Chang'e 4(its name means the moon goddess according to Chinese mythology). Later on CNSA landed on Mars with a new spacecraft Tianwen-1, CNSA was the 2nd company that managed to land on Mars succesfully without any problems(the first company to land on Mars was NASA).
